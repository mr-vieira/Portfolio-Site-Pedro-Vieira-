The Context

No matter the age, motives and walks of life, people are always seeking knowledge and trying to learn new disciplines. But if learning and remembering dozens, perhaps hundreds of new terms and concepts seem daunting, one can think of how overwhelming it is to learn a new language. It requires time, dedication and consistency to learn a new language.

  • Can we explore and understand how people go about learning new vocabulary and languages?
  • What makes them succeed or fail?

The Challenge

How might we design a mobile app that empowers people to learn a new Language?

Competitive_analysis

To get a better understanding of the problem area, and the solutions that have already been created to address it, I conducted a competitive analysis on some of the most well-known apps in this field, namely: Babbel, Duolingo and BBC Learning English. Being around for quite some time, and well-rated in Play Store and App Store, I assumed they must be doing something right.

  • One underexplored aspect in each of these apps is customization.
  • The learning experience is a strict path.
  • Can this formatted and generic approach serve all users in the same way?

Problem Statement

At this point, through research and data analysis, I already had a basic understanding of the competition and target audience. It was now time to consider all learnings and rethink the initial problem statement, this time referring to the User Persona.

Julian needs a Learning Experience tailored to his short and mid-term needs because he wants to start communicating with work colleagues and locals right away, as his work and social life depend highly on it.

Ideation

With a primary Persona, User Stories and a refined Problem Statement, I was now able to think of new functionalities or features aimed at solving real problems for real people.

(1) Custom Study Plan

We Believe that by creating a feature that allows creating a custom study plan, for Julian we’ll achieve a consistent record of tasks and exercises completed over a month, thus reflecting a new learning habit.

(2) Everyday Vocabulary Lesson

We Believe that by giving easy access to categorised chunks of daily vocabulary, for Julian we will provide ways of preparing and applying new learnings according to his immediate needs.

(3) Custom Vocabulary

We Believe that by building a feature that allows Julian to save custom words, phrases and idioms, we will allow Julian to engage with his learning process, create custom content and remember his new learnings in real context.

(4) Pronunciation Classes

We Believe that by creating pronunciation classes with a listen-record-compare feature, for Julian we will achieve a way of correcting himself, being aware of his progress, and gaining confidence when speaking with fluent speakers.

Usability Testing

Putting the prototype to the test.

After recruiting 3 new participants, it was time to put the prototype to the test, and evaluate if the proposed features, Information architecture and navigation were clear and efficient.

To evaluate a participant’s ability to complete specific tasks, I’ve defined 4 Scenario tasks, giving real-world context to usability testing.

Scenario Tasks

(Task 1)

"You’ve decided to learn a new idiom and had just installed a language learning app on your smartphone. Try to create a new account and get to the dashboard."

(Task 2)

"Let’s say that one of your weak spots is pronunciation. How would you use this app to help you with that?"

(Task 3)

"Starting and maintaining new habits, like learning a new idiom, can present a real challenge. Can you use the app to help you establish new habits? How?"

(Task 4)

"Occasionally you read or hear words/expressions that you’d like to keep and not forget. Use the app to save new vocabulary and add your definitions."

Refining the Prototype

Task 1 - Issue (A)

Users were surprised by the sudden appearance of a lesson during the onboarding process.

Proposed Solution

Add a page that introduces the lesson.

Task 1 - Issue (B)

A user got stuck on the 1st Lesson page of the onboarding, there’s not enough information to complete the exercise. Doesn’t feel like a real exercise.

Proposed Solution

Final Thoughts

Key Learnings

As my first experience with a UX design project, the learning by doing approach felt like a good starting point.

Instead of expecting breakthrough ideas, I was focused instead on internalizing the methods, frameworks and mindset of a UX designer.

“Scratching my own itch?”

To get a better understanding of the problem area, and the solutions that have already been created to address it, I conducted a competitive analysis on some of the most well-known apps in this field, namely: Babbel, Duolingo and BBC Learning English. Being around for quite some time, and well-rated in Play Store and App Store, I assumed they must be doing something right.

Collaboration

By approaching this challenge alone, I missed the collaboration aspect of it. From my previous work experience, bouncing ideas with others is one of the best ways of getting new perspectives on a problem and potential ways of solving it.

Next Steps

I intend to continue iterating by submitting the revised prototype to a few more usability testing rounds.

Before moving to high-fidelity prototypes, I would also like to test a few more features related to human interaction, and how students can help each other through their learning journey.
